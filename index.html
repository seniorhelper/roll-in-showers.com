What You Need to Know

What Is a Roll-In Shower — and Why Does It Matter?

A roll-in shower is a zero-threshold, barrier-free shower specifically engineered so a wheelchair can roll straight in with no curb, lip, or step to navigate. Unlike walk-in showers (which still have a low threshold), a true roll-in shower floor is completely flush — or gently sloped — with the surrounding bathroom floor.

For U.S. Veterans

Veterans: Your Roll-In Shower May Be Fully or Partially Covered

If you have a service-connected disability, you may qualify for federal grants that cover the full cost of a roll-in shower installation. Aging Safely Baths has extensive experience guiding veterans through the VA grant process — from paperwork to final inspection.

  • VA HISA Grant — Up to $6,800 Home Improvements and Structural Alterations grant for veterans with service-connected disabilities needing accessible bathroom modifications.
  • SHA Grant — Up to $21,239 Specially Adapted Housing grant for veterans with more severe service-connected disabilities that substantially limit mobility.
  • SAH Grant — Up to $109,986 Special Home Adaptation grant for veterans who need extensive adaptive modifications to live independently at home.

A roll-in shower has zero threshold — the floor is completely flush with no curb or lip, allowing a wheelchair to roll directly in. A walk-in shower typically still has a low 1–4 inch threshold that requires stepping over. Roll-in showers are specifically engineered for wheelchair users and those who cannot step over any barrier, while walk-in showers are great for seniors and those with limited mobility who can still walk.

Yes — in most cases, an existing bathtub space can be converted into a roll-in shower. ADA standards require a minimum 60 inches wide by 30 inches deep for a transfer-type roll-in shower, or 36" x 36" for a standard accessible shower. For full wheelchair maneuverability, a 60 x 36 inch or larger footprint is recommended along with a 60-inch turning radius clearance outside the shower. Additional ADA requirements include grab bar placement, seat height (17–19 inches), showerhead height range, and control placement within reach from the seat. Our team ensures full ADA compliance on every installation.
Most residential roll-in shower installations are completed in one to two days. A straightforward tub conversion in an existing space is typically a one-day project. Larger custom tile installations, floor structure modifications, or commercial projects may take 2–3 days.
